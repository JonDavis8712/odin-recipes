Filipino Adobo Recipe

Filipino adobo,made with chicken or pork, is a classic dish first recorded in 1613. The dish is made with vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, bay leaves and black peppercorns

The original recipe can be traced back to a spanish occupation of the country during the 17th century. A mixture of spanish and local indegenous recipes combined together to create the well known filipino classic

RECIPE

  • 1.5 lb boneless skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/3 cup soy sauce
  • 1/3 cup + 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 1 small brown onion
  • 3 garlic gloves
  • 1 tbsp whole black peppercorns
  • 2 tbsp brown sugar
  • 4 bay leaves
  • 2 green onion
  • 1 1/2 cups water

STEPS

  1. combine chicken and liquid ingredients overnight
  2. heat the oil on a skillet over high heat
  3. add the chicken and sear both sides until browned
  4. remove chicken, skillet and set asside
  5. heat the remaining oil in skilled and add the garlic and onion. Cook for 1 1/2 minutes
  6. add the rest of the liquid ingedients, water, sugar and black pepper. Bring to a simmer, then turn heat down to medium high. let sit for 5 minutes
  7. add in the chicken smooth side down, simmer uncovered for 20 minutes, turning chicken at aroud the 15 minute mark and until sauce reduces down to a thick jam-like syrup
  8. coat chicken in glaze and serve over rice, (cauliflower rice and ginger smashed cucumbers as a healthier alternative)
